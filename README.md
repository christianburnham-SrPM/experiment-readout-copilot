Experiment Analysis Copilot

Transform raw A/B test results into statistically sound product decisions and executive-ready experiment summaries.

Experiment Analysis Copilot was built to explore a question that has become increasingly relevant in the age of AI-assisted product development:

How do you use AI to accelerate decision-making without compromising trust in the underlying analysis?

The result is a decision-support application that combines deterministic statistical analysis, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and evaluation-driven AI workflows to help product teams analyze experiments more efficiently while maintaining confidence in the results.

The Problem

Experiment readouts are often time-consuming to create and difficult to standardize across teams. More importantly, teams frequently struggle with questions such as:

* Is this result statistically significant?
* Is the experiment adequately powered?
* Have we seen similar outcomes before?
* Should we ship, continue running, or reject the change?
* How can findings be communicated clearly to stakeholders?

While large organizations often have dedicated experimentation platforms and analytics teams, many product teams still rely on manual analysis and ad hoc reporting.

I built Experiment Analysis Copilot to explore how AI could streamline this workflow while ensuring that statistical decisions remain grounded in deterministic analysis rather than generated assumptions.

⸻

Product Principles

A core design principle guided the entire project:

Statistical calculations should be deterministic. AI should explain the results, not create them.

All experiment metrics, significance testing, confidence intervals, power calculations, and decision logic are generated by the statistical engine.

The AI layer is responsible for:

* Translating findings into executive-ready language
* Summarizing outcomes
* Surfacing relevant historical experiments
* Explaining recommendations and tradeoffs

This separation reduces hallucination risk and creates a more trustworthy decision-support experience.

Key Capabilities

Experiment Analysis

Users can provide experiment results directly or upload data through a CSV file.

The platform evaluates:

* Conversion lift
* Statistical significance
* Confidence intervals
* Sample Ratio Mismatch (SRM)
* Statistical power

The output includes both a recommended action and the reasoning behind it.

Experiment Memory (RAG)

The application maintains a searchable library of prior experiment readouts.

When generating recommendations, the system retrieves similar historical experiments and incorporates relevant learnings into the final summary.

Example questions include:

* Have we tested something similar before?
* What happened when we introduced urgency messaging?
* Have previous winners maintained performance over time?

Responses are grounded in retrieved experiment records and include source references.

Executive Readout Generation

The platform automatically produces stakeholder-ready summaries including:

* Key findings
* Decision recommendation
* Risks and considerations
* Relevant historical context

Decision Framework

The recommendation engine uses a deterministic policy built around common experimentation best practices.

Possible outcomes include:

Ship

Statistically significant positive result with sufficient confidence.

Do Not Ship

Negative impact, no measurable benefit, or practical equivalence.

Keep Running

Insufficient statistical power to reach a reliable conclusion.

Investigate

Potential data quality concerns, including tracking issues or sample-ratio mismatches.

The goal was to create recommendations that are both statistically defensible and easy for product teams to act upon.

⸻

Evaluation Approach

A major objective of this project was learning how to evaluate AI-powered systems rather than simply building one.

The evaluation framework measures performance across three areas:

Area	Purpose
Decision Accuracy	Validate experiment recommendations
Retrieval Quality	Measure relevance of historical experiment retrieval
Output Faithfulness	Verify generated summaries remain grounded in source data

Results

* Decision Regression Suite: 20/20 scenarios passed
* Retrieval Evaluation: 90% retrieval success rate
* Memo Faithfulness Testing: Included as part of the application evaluation workflow

The evaluation process directly influenced product decisions and surfaced edge cases that would otherwise have reached production.

One notable example involved experiments with effectively zero measurable impact. Early versions of the recommendation framework suggested continuing to collect additional data despite impractical sample size requirements. Evaluation exposed the issue and led to the introduction of a practical-equivalence decision path.

Key Learnings

Building this project reinforced several observations about AI product development:

1. Evaluation is often more valuable than model selection.
2. Deterministic systems and AI systems should each do what they are best at.
3. Retrieval quality can have as much impact on user trust as model quality.
4. Product decisions become significantly stronger when failure modes are documented and measured.
